# The Home Reset Brand Guide

## Brand Snapshot

The Home Reset is a locally owned residential cleaning brand positioned around peace, trust, and intentional living. The brand promise is not just a clean house. It is giving people their space back so they can rest, focus, and enjoy their lives.

Core brand line:

Reset · Rest · Create

Core business description:

Professional residential cleaning for families, working professionals, and households that want a reliable, peaceful, high-quality reset.

## Brand Positioning

### What the brand does

The Home Reset provides residential cleaning services, deep cleans, recurring maintenance cleans, and short-term rental turnover cleans.

### What makes it different

- Calm, lifestyle-centered positioning instead of transactional cleaning language
- Strong emphasis on trust, reliability, and intentional care
- Clean, safe products suitable for families
- A tone that feels personal, steady, and restorative

### Brand promise

We reset your space so you can return to what matters most.

### Emotional outcome

Clients should feel:

- relieved
- cared for
- proud of their home
- able to slow down and breathe
- free to focus on family, work, and personal life

## Audience

Primary audiences reflected in the current site:

- Busy families
- Stay-at-home parents
- Professionals working long days
- Short-term rental owners or hosts

These audiences are united by one need: a dependable home environment that reduces stress and restores order.

## Brand Personality

The brand should feel:

- warm
- grounded
- trustworthy
- refined but not flashy
- peaceful
- capable
- personal
- detail-oriented

The brand should not feel:

- sterile
- corporate
- trendy for the sake of trendiness
- overly luxurious or exclusive
- jokey or casual in a sloppy way
- aggressive or salesy

## Voice And Tone

### Voice

Write in a way that is:

- clear
- calm
- caring
- competent
- lightly elevated

### Tone

The tone can shift slightly by page, but should remain consistent.

- Homepage: warm, aspirational, restorative
- Services: practical, clear, confident
- About: personal, trustworthy, values-led
- Booking and contact: simple, reassuring, direct

### Writing principles

- Lead with peace and transformation, not chores
- Keep copy human and readable
- Use plain language with occasional elevated phrasing
- Emphasize reliability and consistency
- Frame cleaning as support for a better life, not just a cleaner room

### Good message patterns

- We clean so you can live.
- Come home to peace.
- Reset your space.
- Reliable care for the home you live in every day.
- A fresh, peaceful space you can actually enjoy.

### Avoid

- Hard-sell phrases
- Cheap-discount language as the main message
- Overly clinical cleaning jargon
- Generic corporate filler like best-in-class solutions
- Overpromising language that sounds too polished or impersonal

## Messaging Pillars

### 1. Reset creates room for life

Cleaning is framed as restoring order so clients can rest, create, connect, and live well.

### 2. Trust matters

The brand repeatedly signals that cleaners are reliable, background-checked, safe, and respectful of the home.

### 3. Intentional care

The service is not rushed or careless. It is thoughtful, detailed, and family-conscious.

### 4. Consistency over spectacle

The experience is dependable and repeatable. The promise is not luxury theater. It is peace of mind.

## Key Proof Points On The Current Site

- 10+ years of experience
- 500+ happy clients
- 4,200+ cleans completed
- Consistent five-star positioning
- Background-checked cleaners
- Safe, non-toxic products
- Flexible recurring schedules
- Transparent pricing

These are the strongest trust builders and should appear often in future marketing.

## Service Framing

Current service naming and framing:

- The Reset: recurring or standard maintenance clean
- Deep Clean: top-to-bottom, extra-attention clean
- Short Term Rental / AirBnB: turnover cleaning
- Add-ons: practical, optional service extensions

Naming convention guidance:

- Prefer simple, memorable service names
- Keep “The Reset” as the signature offer
- Use plain-English descriptions under branded names

## Visual Identity

### Overall look

The website uses a soft, warm, earthy palette with cream backgrounds, muted tan accents, and dark espresso contrast. The visual mood is calm, restorative, welcoming, and slightly elevated.

### Color palette in use

Primary background colors:

- `--bg`: `#F9F6F1`
- `--bg-alt`: `#F0EAE0`
- `--bg-dark`: `#2A1F14`

Accent colors:

- `--accent`: `#C9A97A`
- `--accent-dark`: `#A8834F`

Text colors:

- `--text`: `#2A1F14`
- `--text-muted`: `#8B7355`

Supporting UI color:

- `--border`: `#E2D9CC`

### Color meaning

- Creams and soft beige: calm, clean, approachable
- Gold-tan accents: warmth, quality, care
- Dark espresso: trust, grounding, contrast

### UI style patterns

- Rounded corners throughout
- Soft shadows instead of harsh outlines
- Warm gradients in hero and section banners
- Calm spacing and generous padding
- Dark CTA sections used sparingly for emphasis

## Typography Direction

The current site uses system sans-serif typography, but the visual brand direction suggests the type should feel:

- clean
- modern
- approachable
- polished without becoming editorial or fashion-heavy

Headline treatment should feel confident and spacious. Supporting text should remain highly readable and never feel dense.

## Layout And Component Guidance

### Preferred patterns

- Large calm hero with strong headline and a restorative message
- Card-based sections for features, values, pricing, and testimonials
- Rounded pill buttons for CTAs and tags
- Alternating warm light and dark sections for rhythm
- Short labels above section headings in uppercase tracking text

### CTA style

Primary CTA language should be direct and warm.

Examples already aligned with the brand:

- Book a Cleaning
- View Full Pricing
- Contact Us
- Book Now
- It’s Free to Inquire

## Photography And Imagery Direction

If photography or social assets are added, they should feel:

- natural light
- warm neutral interiors
- tidy but lived-in homes
- peaceful, breathable, uncluttered environments
- family-safe and welcoming

Avoid imagery that feels:

- cold blue-toned
- overly staged luxury real estate
- industrial or commercial
- packed with cleaning tools as the hero subject

The focus should be the feeling of the home after the reset.

## Brand Do And Don’t

### Do

- speak to peace, relief, and restored order
- emphasize reliability and trust
- use warm, grounded visual tones
- keep layouts open and easy to scan
- connect cleaning to the client’s actual life

### Don’t

- make the brand feel bargain-bin or coupon-led
- lean into harsh before-and-after language too heavily
- use loud colors or cold grayscale palettes
- overcomplicate the service story
- make the tone feel corporate or overly trendy

## Example Brand Copy

### Short elevator version

The Home Reset offers warm, reliable residential cleaning that helps busy households come home to peace.

### SEO-friendly description

The Home Reset provides professional residential cleaning in the Fort Worth and Eagle Mountain area, helping busy families and professionals enjoy a clean, peaceful home with reliable, thoughtful service.

### One-paragraph brand version

The Home Reset is a locally owned residential cleaning company built around trust, consistency, and intentional care. We help busy families, professionals, and hosts reset their homes so they can rest, create, and spend more time on what matters most.

## Future Brand Opportunities

Natural next evolutions for this brand would be:

- a more intentional type system that matches the calm premium tone
- a small logo mark or monogram built around reset, home, or circular renewal
- a fuller photo direction library for social and marketing use
- a short brand story block for bios, flyers, and local listings

## Summary

The Home Reset brand is best understood as warm residential care, not generic cleaning. Its strongest identity is the connection between a reset home and a better life. Every future design, page, ad, and caption should preserve that feeling.
